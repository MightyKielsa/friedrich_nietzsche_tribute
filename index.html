Friedrich Nietzsche

The mustache who conquered nihilism

Coloured and enhanced photograph of Friedrich Nietzsche

Nietzsche was a German philosopher, cultural critic and philologist whose work has exerted a profound influence on modern intellectual history. Here is a short timeline of the great philosophers life:

  • 1844 - Friedrich Nietzsche was born in a small german villiage known as Rocken bei Lutzen.
  • 1849 - His father died with a minor brain ailment when Nietzsche was barely five years old.
  • 1850 - After his youger brother dies him and his sister move to live with Nietzsche's paternal Grandmother.
  • 1856 - His Grandmother died and the family had to move back to their own house.
  • 1858 - Friedrich was admitted to Schulpforta School, where he was exposed to ancient Greek and Roman literature. He also worked on poems and music compositions during this time
  • 1864 - He graduates from respected Schulpforta
  • 1865 - Enrolles at University of Leipzig
  • 1867 - He signs up for one year voluntary military service
  • 1868 - Meets and becomes friends with Richard Wagner
  • 1869 - Nietzsche renounces his Prussian citizenship and remains stateless. The same year he becomes a professor of classical philology at University of Basel.
  • 1872 - "The Birth of Tragedy" is the first book the young philosopher releases
  • 1878 - He releases another book, "Human, All Too Human". This book marks the begining of the development of his characteristic aphorisic style of writing
  • 1883 - Publishes one of his greatest works: "Thus Spoke Zarathustra"
  • 1886 - Nietzsche writes another philosophical masterpiece "Beyond Good and Evil" in which he exopands on concepts presented in his previous work
  • 1888 - He writes "Twilight of the Idols" in just over a week as a self-proclaimed introduction to his philosophy
  • 1888 - Later the same year he writes "Ecce Homo" which is a reflection on his own previous work, the significance of his philosophy in the larger context and his development. It wasn't published until after his death.
  • 1889 - Nietzsche suffers a severe mental breakdown and his mental health deteriorates rapidly
  • 1890 - He's taken into care by his mother
  • 1898 - Friedrich Nietzsche suffers a stroke which leaves him partially paralysed
  • 1900 - This year marks the death of the philosopher after having another stroke
